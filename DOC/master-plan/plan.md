The site should communicate institutional credibility, make product discovery easy across multiple commodity sectors, and convert serious visitors into qualified commercial conversations.

## 2. Page-by-Page Breakdown

### Home

Purpose:
Establish credibility within seconds, communicate sector breadth, and route visitors into the correct commercial path.

Audience intent:
Buyers want reliable supply, suppliers want credible offtake, and partners want proof of operational scale.

Section structure:
- Hero with value proposition
  Content: headline centered on reliable global commodity supply, short support copy, primary CTA for Request a Quote, secondary CTA for Explore Products, credibility strip with traded sectors, regions served, and years of experience.
- Trust signals
  Content: certifications, compliance commitments, origin and destination coverage, financing and logistics strengths, client types served.
- Sector overview
  Content: four sector cards for Agriculture, Energy, Fertilizers, and Industrial Materials with short commercial summaries and links into category hubs.
- Why work with us
  Content: sourcing network, quality control, logistics execution, risk-managed delivery, and documentation support.
- Featured trade corridors
  Content: visual map or structured list showing major origin-to-destination lanes, supported by port access and modal capabilities.
- Capabilities snapshot
  Content: summaries for origination, shipping, quality assurance, trade finance, and market intelligence.
- Featured insights
  Content: latest market notes, trade updates, and case-led proof of execution.
- Conversion band
  Content: segmented CTA modules for buyers, suppliers, and strategic partners, each with a tailored entry point.
- Footer
  Content: regional contact info, compliance links, utility navigation, and a consistent copyright statement.

### About

Purpose:
Translate corporate legitimacy into a clear story about operating model, geographic reach, and standards.

Audience intent:
Visitors want proof that the company is stable, serious, compliant, and globally capable.

Section structure:
- Brand overview
  Content: concise company narrative, mission, and operating thesis.
- Leadership and governance
  Content: senior profiles, governance principles, decision-making credibility, and commercial leadership coverage.
- Global footprint
  Content: office locations, trade lanes, warehouses, partner ports, and regional coverage.
- Compliance and ethics
  Content: sanctions compliance, anti-bribery stance, documentation discipline, and audit readiness.
- Sustainability and sourcing standards
  Content: responsible sourcing commitments, supplier screening approach, and environmental or social priorities.
- CTA
  Content: prompt to speak with the commercial team or download the company profile.

  Content: adjacent grades, substitutes, and related commodity families.

## 3. Product Architecture

### Product organization model

The site should use a three-level commercial taxonomy:

1. Sector level
   Agriculture, Energy, Fertilizers, Industrial Materials.
2. Commodity family level
   Example: Agriculture -> Grains -> Wheat, Corn, Barley.
3. Product detail level
   Individual commercial pages for each traded product, grade, or key specification family.

### Taxonomy rules

- Organize first by how buyers search and buy, not by internal org chart.
- Use subcategories only when they matter commercially, technically, or operationally.
- Avoid thin pages for every minor grade; group close variants when the commercial story is the same.
- Use faceted attributes inside product pages and filters for grade, origin, packaging, application, and destination suitability.

### Recommended product data model

Each product should support the following fields:

- Product name
- Sector
- Family and subfamily
- Short commercial summary
- Applications
- Grades and variants
- Technical specifications table
- Origins
- Available packaging formats
- Shipment modes
- Supported incoterms
- Minimum order guidance
- Lead-time range
- Documents available
- Quality or certification notes
- Related products
- Inquiry routing tags

### Product detail page template

Required blocks:

- Commercial headline and quick facts
- Technical specifications table
- Industry applications
- Origin and supply continuity overview
- Logistics and trade terms
- Inspection and quality controls
- FAQ specific to the product
- Qualified inquiry form
- Related products and resources

### Expansion strategy

- Add new sectors without changing navigation logic by preserving the sector -> family -> product structure.
- Create market-specific landing pages only when there is distinct regulatory, logistics, or demand context.
- Support gated spec sheets and downloadable documents for high-value products where procurement teams need offline review.
- Allow insight articles and case studies to connect directly to relevant product families.

## 4. Conversion Strategy & User Flows

### Primary conversion goals

- Request a Quote from qualified buyers
- Submit a Sourcing Request for hard-to-source or multi-origin requirements
- Register as a Supplier
- Start a Strategic Partnership discussion
- Subscribe to market updates for nurture and remarketing

### Conversion architecture

- Primary CTA across the site: Request a Quote
- Secondary CTA by context: Talk to Trade Desk, Explore Products, Become a Supplier, Download Company Profile
- Persistent header CTA: Request a Quote
- Sticky mobile CTA: Contact Trade Desk
- Product pages: embedded inquiry form above the fold and again near the logistics section
- Insights pages: subscribe CTA and related commercial CTA tied to the content topic

### User flow: buyer

1. Lands on home, product page, or SEO article.
2. Confirms company credibility via trust signals and market coverage.
3. Navigates to relevant sector, family, and product page.
4. Reviews specifications, origins, and commercial terms.
5. Submits quote request with volume, destination, and timeline.
6. Lead is routed to the correct regional or sector sales owner.

### User flow: supplier

1. Lands on Partner With Us or market-specific sourcing page.
2. Reviews supplier standards, markets of interest, and onboarding expectations.
3. Completes supplier registration form with product, origin, certifications, and capacity.
4. Internal team qualifies and routes the lead for onboarding.

### User flow: strategic partner

1. Lands on Capabilities, Markets, or Partner With Us.
2. Understands business model and partnership lanes such as logistics, storage, finance, or distribution.
3. Reviews proof of execution and network footprint.
4. Submits a partnership discussion request.

### Form strategy

- Use short forms for early-stage inquiries on high-traffic pages.
- Use progressive qualification on product and partnership forms.
- Require business email, company name, product interest, volume range, destination market, and timeline on quote forms.
- Use dropdown logic to route by commodity sector and geography.
- Add optional document upload for supplier and partnership forms.
- Show clear response expectations to increase trust and reduce low-quality submissions.

## 6. SEO & Growth Strategy

### SEO structure

The SEO model should combine commercial pages and authority content.

- Commercial SEO layer
  Sector pages, family pages, product pages, origin pages, destination pages, and capability pages.
- Authority SEO layer
  Insights, guides, explainers, market updates, and case studies.
- Conversion SEO layer
  Intent-driven landing pages such as bulk supplier for [commodity], [commodity] exporter, [commodity] importer, and [region] commodity trading partner.

### Core keyword clusters

- Commodity product terms
  Example: wheat supplier, urea fertilizer exporter, industrial sulfur supplier.
- Origin and destination terms
  Example: corn exporter from Brazil, fertilizer supplier to East Africa.
- Capability terms
  Example: commodity logistics partner, global sourcing and trade finance.
- Buyer education terms
  Example: incoterms for bulk commodity trade, how to source industrial raw materials.

### Content cluster recommendations

- Agriculture cluster
  Grain sourcing, quality standards, seasonal supply, feed ingredient trade.
- Energy cluster
  regional energy product movement, compliance considerations, storage and shipping notes.
- Fertilizer cluster
  crop-cycle demand, nutrient comparisons, import timing, bulk handling.
- Industrial materials cluster
  industrial applications, spec selection, sourcing risk, and supply continuity.
- Trade operations cluster
  incoterms, letters of credit, inspections, demurrage, shipping documentation, and compliance topics.

### Growth engine

- Publish recurring market updates tied to the traded sectors.
- Build evergreen resource pages that answer procurement questions.
- Use destination-market pages to win regional B2B search demand.
- Connect insights to product pages through contextual CTAs and internal links.
- Capture email subscribers by sector to create segmented nurture streams.

### Technical SEO priorities

- Clean URL structure based on the sector/family/product hierarchy.
- Unique metadata and schema markup for product, article, organization, and FAQ content.
- Strong internal linking from insights to products, capabilities, and market pages.
- Fast page performance with optimized media and server-side rendering.
- Canonical handling for similar product variants and filtered states.

## 7. Differentiation & Strategic Recommendations

### How this site stands out

Most commodity trading websites are generic, static, and self-congratulatory. This site should differentiate by being more operationally transparent, more commercially useful, and more structurally specific.

### Strategic recommendations

#### 1. Build around trade execution proof, not vague corporate claims
Show trade corridors, sourcing origins, quality processes, and documentation standards. Buyers trust execution detail more than slogans.

#### 2. Use the Trust Center as a conversion asset
Centralizing certifications, policies, and document standards will shorten due diligence cycles and increase buyer confidence.

#### 3. Make products commercially informative, not catalog-like
Every product page should combine technical data, application context, and logistics guidance so the page helps a real procurement conversation move forward.

#### 4. Turn geography into a commercial advantage
Markets and origins content should prove regional fluency and improve SEO at the same time.

#### 5. Segment conversion paths by audience type
Do not force buyers, suppliers, and partners through the same messaging or form flow. Each group needs dedicated context and qualification.

#### 6. Use insights to support both brand and pipeline
Editorial content should not be a blog for its own sake. It should reinforce market expertise, rank for strategic search queries, and route qualified readers into commercial CTAs.

#### 7. Design for scale from day one
Use reusable templates for product families, product pages, insight articles, origin pages, and corridor pages so the site can grow without structural rework.

### Final strategic direction

The strongest version of this website is not a polished brochure. It is a commercial platform that proves credibility, accelerates qualification, and turns sector expertise into measurable business conversations. If the site consistently answers three questions - what do you trade, where can you deliver, and why should we trust you - it will outperform the category average both in conversion quality and long-term search growth.
